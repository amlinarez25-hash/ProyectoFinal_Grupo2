Weekly Recipes


At Weekly Cup, we love putting a twist on classic drinks.
Every week, we bring you easy and creative recipes for you to enjoy at home.

Today, we're experimenting with matcha, taro, and chai, but in combinations that are sure to surprise you.

Blueberry Matcha
Latte


If you already love the classic matcha latte, you'll love this blueberry version. The fresh, slightly tangy flavor of the blueberries blends perfectly with the earthiness of the matcha, creating a colorful and delicious drink.


Ingredients:
  • 1 teaspoon matcha
  • 2 tablespoons blueberry puree (homemade or light jam)
  • 1 cup milk (whole or plant-based)
  • 1–2 teaspoons honey or sugar
  • Ice to taste
How to do it:

First, you have to dissolve the matcha in a little bit of hot water. In a glass, place the blueberry puree and add the cup of milk. Then, pour the matcha, do it slowly so it looks layered. Sweeten to your liking, and voila: an Instagram-worthy latte.

Coconut Taro
Milk Tea


Taro is already famous for its beautiful lilac color, but if you mix it with coconut milk, it becomes even more tropical and creamy. It's like taking a vacation with every sip.


Ingredients:
  • 2 tablespoons taro powder
  • ½ cup hot water
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1–2 teaspoons sugar or honey
  • Tapioca pearls and ice (optional)
How to do it:

Dissolve the taro powder in hot water, add the coconut milk, and mix well. Sweeten, add ice, and, if you're feeling adventurous, some tapioca pearls. The result: a sweet, creamy, and super delicious drink.

Vanilla Chai
Latte


Chai already has that spicy note that embraces you like autumn in a cup. But if you add vanilla, it becomes a comforting, smooth, and aromatic beverage. Perfect for a relaxing end to the week.


Ingredients:
  • 1 chai tea bag
  • ½ cup hot water
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract or syrup
  • Honey or sugar to taste
  • Cinnamon to decorate
How to do it:

To start, prepare the chai tea with hot water and let it steep for a few minutes. Heat the milk with vanilla and mix it into the tea. Sweeten to taste and sprinkle a little cinnamon on top. Serve hot and enjoy the moment.
